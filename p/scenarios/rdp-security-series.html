RDP Brute Force & Hardening Case Study

End-to-end purple-team lifecycle for RDP (TCP 3389): baseline brute force simulation, EchoSentinel detection engineering (rules, correlation, tuning), remediation/hardening, and final validation retest. Focus is explainable detections backed by evidence.

Purple Team Detection Engineering Incident Response Remediation Control Validation RDP / 3389 EchoSentinel Windows Event Logs MITRE ATT&CK

Key Findings

  • Root cause: exposed RDP surface with weak access scoping enabled repeated credential guessing.
  • Detection reality: single-event alerts (one 4625) are noisy; confidence requires thresholds + context.
  • Correlation: repeated 4625 (LogonType 10) followed by 4624 indicates likely compromise path.
  • Remediation impact: scoping + lockout + NLA/TLS reduces exposure and changes attacker economics.
  • Outcome: EchoSentinel surfaces high-confidence RDP brute patterns with evidence pivots.

Detection Design

Signal Chain

RDP detection confidence is built on RDP-specific logon telemetry (LogonType=10), thresholds, and correlation of compromise patterns.

  • 4625 — failed logons (LogonType 10 for RDP)
  • 4740 — account lockout (mitigation confirmation + attack indicator)
  • 4624 — success after failures (compromise candidate)
  • Correlation: fails → success within 10 minutes
Rules Created Tuning Notes

Why This Works

  • Uses LogonType parsing to avoid generic auth noise.
  • Thresholds reduce “one-off fail” false positives.
  • Correlation removes ambiguity: failures alone are common; failures then success is high-signal.
  • Validation retest proves controls changed the outcome, not just the alerting.
